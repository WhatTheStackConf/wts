# WTS Product Context

## Register

product

## Product Purpose

WhatTheStack is the operational and public web app for the WTS 2026 conference. The admin area helps organizers manage conference operations: users, CFP submissions, reviewers, speakers, sessions, partners, tickets, and publication state.

## Users

- Admins and organizers who need to move quickly through operational work without second guessing state, save behavior, or publication impact.
- Review leads who need trustworthy proposal, scoring, and weight views.
- Content and partnership coordinators who create public-facing records as drafts, then publish when ready.

## Jobs To Be Done

- Create and edit records safely, especially public speakers, sessions, sponsors, and partners.
- See publication state at a glance and change it intentionally.
- Move accepted CFP applicants into public speaker profiles.
- Diagnose missing data, empty states, sync states, and errors without leaving the admin workflow.
- Preserve a clear boundary between private CFP/review content and public programme content.

## Brand And Tone

WTS is technical, high-energy, and conference-native. The visual language can feel cyberpunk and system-access oriented, but the admin area must stay legible, consistent, and trustworthy because users are doing real operational work.

Tone words: cyberpunk, precise, high-trust.

## Quality Bar

Flagship polish. The admin area should feel intentionally designed and production-grade, not merely functional.

## Strategic Principles

- Product clarity beats decoration. Keep the cyberpunk identity, but never let effects obscure forms, tables, status, or actions.
- Draft and Published states must be unmistakable, consistent, and reversible.
- Forms use visible labels, useful hints, native constraints, clear required indicators, and delayed validation feedback.
- Admin screens should share one component vocabulary for shells, panels, buttons, filters, forms, tables, empty states, loading states, and alerts.
- Public-site data boundaries matter. Admin copy must distinguish CFP Submissions, Speakers, and Sessions using the domain language in `CONTEXT.md`.

## Anti-References

- Generic SaaS admin pages with bland white cards and no WTS identity.
- Overloaded hacker dashboards where neon effects compete with fields and data.
- Placeholder-only forms, mismatched buttons, inconsistent spacing, and tables that feel assembled screen by screen.
- Decorative glass effects used as the default answer instead of clear hierarchy.
